# 👥 User Research Summary - Solo Accounting

This document summarizes our target audience research, user archetypes, pain points with existing financial platforms, and core user workflows that shape Solo Accounting's product requirements.

---

## 🎯 Target Audience

Our primary users are **independent operators and small business owners** who manage their own books. They are not professional accountants, and they often find bookkeeping to be the most stressful and time-consuming aspect of running their business.

* **Freelancers & Solopreneurs:** Creative designers, copywriters, software contractors, and remote consultants.
* **Gig Economy Workers:** Rideshare drivers, delivery partners, couriers, and independent tutors.
* **Micro-Businesses (1-3 Employees):** Small boutique shops, local service providers, and family-owned businesses.
* **Small Businesses (<10 Employees):** General contractors, service businesses (plumbing, electrical), and small retail/e-commerce firms.
  > [!NOTE]
  > Small businesses with up to 10 employees are fair game for Solo Accounting. At this scale, the owner is still actively involved in the day-to-day books and feels the high licensing fees and complexity of enterprise software intensely.

---

## 🎭 User Personas

To guide our product design and engineering decisions, we have developed five representative user personas. These profiles highlight the diversity of workflows, software pain points, and administrative costs across our target market:

### 1. [Sarah Baker (Artisanal Bakery Owner)](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/2_user_research/sarah_baker.md)
* **Archetype:** Solopreneur / Passionate Maker
* **Income:** ~$45,000 gross
* **Core Pain Point:** Pricing transparency, separating business/personal bank accounts, and manual receipt collection stress.
* **Labor & Cost:** 6 hours/month bookkeeping + $1,400/year CPA estimated tax review.

### 2. [Joe Plumber (Residential Plumbing Operator)](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/2_user_research/joe_plumber.md)
* **Archetype:** Tradesperson / Active Trade Owner
* **Income:** ~$240,000 gross (3 apprentices)
* **Core Pain Point:** In-field invoicing leakage, cash flow visibility, and bi-weekly employee payroll tax complexity.
* **Labor & Cost:** 12 hours/month bookkeeping/payroll + $2,440/year in services and software.

### 3. [Elena Designer (Remote Brand Consultant)](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/2_user_research/elena_designer.md)
* **Archetype:** Freelancer / Digital Nomad Contractor
* **Income:** ~$85,000 gross (international clients)
* **Core Pain Point:** Hidden foreign exchange fees on invoices, scattered digital receipt archives, and unintegrated time tracking.
* **Labor & Cost:** 4 hours/month bookkeeping + $750/year in tools and consultations.

### 4. [Marcus Contractor (Expanding Construction Firm)](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/2_user_research/marcus_contractor.md)
* **Archetype:** Small Business Owner (8 crew members)
* **Income:** ~$650,000 gross
* **Core Pain Point:** Paying for massive, bloated commercial software suites just to get simple multi-employee payroll and seat access.
* **Labor & Cost:** 20 hours/month bookkeeping/payroll + $7,700/year in accounting systems and services.

### 5. [Diana Gigworker (Multi-App Courier)](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/2_user_research/diana_gigworker.md)
* **Archetype:** Gig Economy Operator / Contractor
* **Income:** ~$32,000 gross
* **Core Pain Point:** Tax time shock, lack of mileage/deduction tracking, and a blind spot around vehicle depreciation.
* **Labor & Cost:** 8 hours/month logging + $150/year filing software + tax penalty risks.
