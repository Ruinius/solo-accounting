# ⚔️ Competitor Analysis: Wave Accounting

This file contains a detailed, citation-backed teardown of **Wave Accounting**, a highly popular cloud-based accounting platform known for its historically free core pricing model.

---

## 📌 Specific Product Name
*   **Product Name:** Wave Accounting
*   **Developer:** Wave Financial Inc. (a subsidiary of H&R Block)
*   **Hosting Model:** Cloud-only.

---

## 📋 Feature List
*   **Unlimited Invoicing:** Custom invoicing, professional templates, automated recurring billing, and payment reminders [1].
*   **Expense & Income Tracking:** Basic financial ledger recording, transaction matching, and manual CSV imports [1][2].
*   **Financial Reporting:** Generates standard reports, including Profit & Loss (P&L), Balance Sheet, and sales tax reports [1][2].
*   **Receipt Scanning (OCR):** Available natively on the Pro tier to extract info from photo receipts [1][4].
*   **Team Collaboration:** Supports up to 2 collaborators on the free plan, and unlimited users on the Pro tier [1].

---

## 💰 Price
*   **Starter Plan (Free Tier):** **$0.00 / month** [1][3]. Includes unlimited invoicing and expense tracking, but limits bank imports to **manual imports** only [1][2].
*   **Pro Plan:** **$19.00 / month** (or $190/year) [1]. Unlocks automated bank feed synchronization and native OCR receipt scanning [1][3].
*   **Payment Processing Fees:** Monetized via credit card transaction fees (2.9% + $0.60 per Visa/MC transaction; 3.4% + $0.60 for Amex; 1% for ACH bank transfers) [1][3].
*   **Payroll Add-on:** $40.00 / month base fee + $6.00 / month per active employee [1][3].
*   **Citations:** Wave Financial Official Pricing Matrix, 2026.

---

## 🎯 Target Audience
*   **Primary Users:** Small business owners, freelancers, consultants, and service-based solopreneurs who require elegant invoicing and bookkeeping on a budget and wish to avoid expensive monthly subscriptions [1][3].

---

## 🎨 User Experience (UX)
*Visual Reference:* ![Wave Accounting Dashboard UI](file:///f:/AIML%20projects/solo-accounting/3_competitive_research/screenshots/wave_accounting.png)

*   **UI Analysis:** Wave is widely celebrated for its clean, beautiful, and highly approachable SaaS interface:
    *   Features a pleasant, airy color palette with soft gray backgrounds and subtle teal/blue accent elements.
    *   A minimalist sidebar panel organizing sections clearly.
    *   A simplified, distraction-free invoicing setup that looks and feels highly professional.
*   **UX Strengths:** Extremely modern, elegant, and friendly to beginners. Invoices can be set up and sent within minutes.
*   **UX Weaknesses:** The dashboard incorporates persistent, subtle ads for H&R Block services and Wave's paid payroll and payment gateways.

---

## ⭐️ User Ratings and Reviews
*   **Capterra Rating:** **~4.4 / 5 stars** (based on over 1,700 reviews) [1].
*   **G2 Rating:** **~4.4 / 5 stars** (based on over 300 reviews) [2].
*   **Key Pro Sentiment:**
    *   **Excellent Value:** The free invoice generation and unlimited bookkeeping features on the Starter tier are highly praised as the best free offering in the industry [1].
    *   **Aesthetic & Ease of Use:** Users love the clean dashboard design and automated payment reminders, which help freelancers get paid faster [1].
*   **Key Con Sentiment:**
    *   **Feature Paywalls:** Recent monetization shifts—such as removing automatic bank feeds from the free tier and placing them behind the $19/mo Pro plan—have drawn severe criticism from long-time free users [1].
    *   **Customer Support Limitations:** Free-tier users have highly restricted support options (primarily self-service help centers and chatbot-only assistance), making complex troubleshooting difficult [1].
*   **Citations:** Capterra and G2 software reviews for Wave Financial (2026).
